THE IDIOT’S GUIDE TO ZAMBIA

 

 

**Zambia is built to be used by folks with a really strong background in systems like this… Since I have no experience at all with systems like this I have had to come up with a very simplistic approach that will help you get started.

 

 

Signing in

Go to the website, NELAonline.org.  Click on Programming on the right hand side under Fetish Fair Fleamarket.  Then click on the option for Presenter Log In.

You will have been assigned a Badge ID and a password .  Use these to login to Zambia.

 

Building in a Volunteer into Zambia

Choose ‘Manage Participants & Schedule’ from the bar across the top.  Click on ‘Enter Participants’.  We need to fill in the first name and the last name.  Then the space for ‘Name for Publication’ and ‘Badge Name’ are both filled in with the name the volunteer wants on their nametag.

Use the drop down menu next to ‘Will Participate and Attend’ to choose ‘YES’

Then fill in the volunteer’s email address and put ‘email’ as the preferred way to be contacted.  Check off the spot for ‘General Volunteers’ and then go to the bottom of the page and hit ‘save’.

You should receive a message that the participant information has been successfully saved.  

DO NOT use the same name on the nametag for multiple volunteers.  For example, when we have 3 different ‘Amanda’s we NEED to have a last initial or something to tell the difference between them.

 

 

Editing information for a Volunteer already built into Zambia

Choose ‘Manage Participants & Schedule’ from the bar across the top.  Click on ‘Administer Participants’.  You can use the drop down lists to locate the volunteer by first name, last name or name on the nametag.  Submit information and drop down to the blue text to choose ‘Edit (volunteer’s name) further.  This will bring up the same screen that we use to enter the volunteers information and you can edit what you wish on this.  This is the way to change the name on the nametag or change the email they want to use. You must go to the bottom and hit ‘save’ in order for the changes to be registered.

 

If they decide they are not going to volunteer after all and you wish to remove them completely from the list of active volunteers you go to ‘will participate and attend’ and choose ‘NO’.

 

**If someone who volunteered last year wants to volunteer again this year, you will not need to go as far as ‘edit further’.  Once you search for, and bring up the page for a volunteer you simply go to the drop down box for ‘Participant is interested and available to participate in FFF #40’ and change it from blank to ‘yes’. Click ‘update’ and that person is now available to be built into sessions.

 

To Build a Volunteer into the Schedule

The easiest way to put someone on the schedule for a shift is to go to ‘Manage Participants & Schedule’ and choose ‘assign participants to a session’. Pick the shift from the ‘select session’ drop down and then click ‘select session’.

Go to the bottom of the page where it says ‘Assign participant not indicated as interested or invited’ and choose the volunteer from the drop down.  Click ‘add’.

To schedule for another shift? Just go to the top of the screen and pick a different shift from the drop down and click ‘select session’ again.  Repeat above steps.

 

 

To Remove a Volunteer from a Session

Go into a session as if you were going to schedule a volunteer.  Drop down to the list of volunteers in the middle of the page.  Remove the check from in front of ‘volunteer’ for the person you are removing, and  click on ‘update’.  Then put a check in front of ‘not interested’ and click on ‘update’. If you try to do both at once it will not work.

 

 

 

The Email Account; Dealing with the Volunteers

 

The email address that we use to have volunteers send their requests is;  NELAvolunteer@nelaonline.org.

In order to get to this email account, go to Gmail and the user id is NELAvolunteer and the password is Interim00

 

I have tried to put a label on emails that I know we are waiting for information on, or ones that need changes.  Once a volunteer is scheduled and there is nothing left to do  change the label to ‘completed request’ and move it to the completed request folder.

 

If someone wants to volunteer, the minimum amount of information we need is their real name, first and last if possible, the name they want on their nametag and the session they want to volunteer for.  If they are sending an email for multiple people we need the info for everyone, and the email addresses for everyone else.  (obviously we get their own address from the email itself)

If they just offer to volunteer and have not chosen a time please refer them to the grid to pick their own jobs and times.  Use something like; “Please go to our website, NELAonline.org and follow the links to volunteering at FFF38.  This will show you the grid of available jobs and times that we need help with.  Just let us know what you would like to do and when and we will be happy to build you in.”

I do not want us choosing their jobs and times as it almost guarantees that they will have a conflict and need a change, most likely at a point where it will cause the most amount of problems.

 

In the ‘drafts’ folder is a generic confirmation that they have been scheduled.  Use it as a base and fill in the sessions you scheduled them for and the name on their nametag.
